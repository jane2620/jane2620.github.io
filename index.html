I'm a second-year Master's student in Computer Science at Princeton University advised by Aleksandra Korolova, working in the Center for Information Technology Policy.

My research centers around the reliability, fairness, and privacy of AI systems. My goal is to evaluate their social impacts and improve their trustworthiness, drawing upon methods from measurement science, statistics, and machine learning. I hope my work can inform both technical and policy decisions. My work has been published in venues such as ACM FAccT and AIES.

I recently started a blog, Eclectic Notes on AI with Aleksandra Korolova. Check it out!
